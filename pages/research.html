Research

Here is a collection of some of the research I’ve done over the past few years, both as part of my Honours degree and for other research projects throughout my time at University.

The Breit interaction

I completed my Honours thesis under the supervision of Dr. Benjamin Roberts in 2024/2025 on the Breit interaction in atomic physics. The Breit interaction represents the leading order relativistic correction to the typical electron-electron (Coulomb) interaction, and in my project I included it into all-orders perturbative methods in atomic physics. In my thesis I also included frequency-dependent corrections to the Breit interaction into the Hartree-Fock procedure, which hadn’t been done before. Edge modes in vortex matter of superfluids

Over the summer of 2023/2024, I completed a summer research project under the supervision of Dr. Matthew Reeves and Professor Matthew Davis in Bose-Einstein condensation. The aim of my project was to try to numerically reproduce the solitonic modes on the edges of bulk superfluid vortex matter predicted by Bogatskiy and Wiegmann. While I never managed to convincingly find them through numerical simulations, I learnt a lot of interesting physics along the way and made a presentation of what I had achieved along the way!
